About

Daniel is focused on applying data-driven analysis techniques to inform policy, design, and development decisions. In particular, he wants to use his skills to enhance urban sustainability and resilience.

Daniel is currently a Postdoctoral Associate at the Center for Urban Science and Progress (CUSP) at New York University. As part of the Building Informatics research team at CUSP, He has been analyzing and modeling building energy and water usage in collaboration with numerous public and private stakeholders with the goal of enhancing energy efficiency and mitigating greenhouse gas emissions.

In September, Daniel will be beginning an AAAS Science and Technology Fellowship in The Department of Homeland Security's Science & Technology Directorate
